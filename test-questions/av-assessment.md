## SECTION 1: CODES & STANDARDS (10 Questions)

### Entry Level

**Q1.** What organization publishes standards for professional AV system design and installation?
- A) NFPA
- B) AVIXA (formerly InfoComm)
- C) IEEE
- D) UL

**Answer:** B) AVIXA (formerly InfoComm)

---

**Q2.** What NEC article covers audio system wiring in commercial installations?
- A) NEC Article 640 (Audio Signal Processing Equipment)
- B) NEC Article 725
- C) NEC Article 800
- D) NEC Article 760

**Answer:** A) NEC Article 640

---

**Q3.** What does "CTS" certification stand for in the AV industry?
- A) Certified Technical Specialist
- B) Certified Technology Specialist
- C) Commercial Technical Standards
- D) Certified Television Systems

**Answer:** B) Certified Technology Specialist (AVIXA certification)

---

### Intermediate Level

**Q4.** According to AVIXA standards, what is the recommended maximum viewing distance for a display using the 4/6/8 rule for "analytical" viewing?
- A) 4× screen height
- B) 6× screen height
- C) 8× screen height
- D) 10× screen height

**Answer:** A) 4× screen height for analytical (detailed data viewing)

---

**Q5.** What ANSI/AVIXA standard addresses audio coverage uniformity (AUCU) specifications?
- A) ANSI/AVIXA V202.01
- B) ANSI/AVIXA A102.01
- C) ANSI/INFOCOMM 1M-2009
- D) ANSI/AVIXA F502.01

**Answer:** B) ANSI/AVIXA A102.01 - Audio Coverage Uniformity

---

**Q6.** Per HDMI 2.1 specification, what is the maximum bandwidth supported?
- A) 18 Gbps
- B) 32 Gbps
- C) 48 Gbps
- D) 72 Gbps

**Answer:** C) 48 Gbps

---

### Advanced Level

**Q7.** What AVIXA standard defines display image size (DIS) for determining proper screen dimensions?
- A) ANSI/AVIXA V202.01
- B) ANSI/INFOCOMM 3M-2011
- C) DISCAS standard
- D) AVIXA Display Sizing Tool

**Answer:** A) ANSI/AVIXA V202.01 - Display Image Size

---

**Q8.** What is the SMPTE 2110 standard used for in professional AV?
- A) Home theater calibration
- B) Professional media transport over IP (uncompressed video/audio)
- C) Consumer HDMI specifications
- D) Projection screen standards

**Answer:** B) Professional media transport over IP

---

**Q9.** According to ADA Section 508, what is required for audio visual systems in federal facilities?
- A) Minimum 85 dB output
- B) Accessibility including closed captioning, assistive listening, and accessible controls
- C) Projection only, no displays
- D) English language only

**Answer:** B) Accessibility features required

---

**Q10.** What IEC standard defines loudspeaker sensitivity measurement methods?
- A) IEC 60268-5
- B) IEC 62368-1
- C) IEC 60950
- D) IEC 61000

**Answer:** A) IEC 60268-5 - Sound system equipment: Loudspeakers

---

## SECTION 2: INSTALLATION (10 Questions)

### Entry Level

**Q1.** What connector type is standard for professional balanced audio connections?
- A) RCA
- B) 3.5mm TRS
- C) XLR
- D) 1/4" TS

**Answer:** C) XLR

---

**Q2.** When installing HDMI cables, what is the recommended maximum distance for passive cable?
- A) 10 feet
- B) 25 feet
- C) 50 feet
- D) 100 feet

**Answer:** C) 50 feet (15m) typical maximum for passive HDMI

---

**Q3.** What is the correct pin configuration for T568B Ethernet termination used in AV-over-IP?
- A) Green, Blue, Orange, Brown pairs
- B) Orange-White, Orange, Green-White, Blue, Blue-White, Green, Brown-White, Brown
- C) Any order as long as both ends match
- D) Manufacturer specific

**Answer:** B) Orange-White, Orange, Green-White, Blue, Blue-White, Green, Brown-White, Brown

---

### Intermediate Level

**Q4.** When installing a projector on a ceiling mount, what structural consideration is critical?
- A) Aesthetic appearance
- B) Load rating of mount and ceiling support structure
- C) Wire color
- D) Remote control line of sight

**Answer:** B) Load rating of mount and ceiling support structure

---

**Q5.** What is the recommended mounting height for a 55" display in a conference room for standing presenter visibility?
- A) Bottom of screen at 24" AFF
- B) Center of screen at eye level (approximately 48-54" AFF)
- C) Top of screen at 96" AFF
- D) Any height is acceptable

**Answer:** B) Center at approximate eye level for primary viewing

---

**Q6.** When running HDMI cables through conduit with other cables, what separation is recommended?
- A) No separation needed
- B) Separate conduit for HDMI or shielded cables
- C) 12" minimum separation
- D) Only run in metal conduit

**Answer:** B) Separate conduit or use shielded HDMI to prevent interference

---

### Advanced Level

**Q7.** When installing a distributed audio system, what impedance configuration uses 70V or 100V systems vs. standard?
- A) Low-impedance (4-8Ω) for short runs; high-impedance (70/100V) for long distributed runs
- B) No difference
- C) 70V for home, 100V for commercial
- D) Based on speaker brand

**Answer:** A) Low-impedance short local; 70/100V for distributed with transformers

---

**Q8.** What fiber optic connector type is commonly used for professional HDMI/DisplayPort extenders?
- A) ST
- B) SC
- C) LC duplex
- D) MPO

**Answer:** C) LC duplex (common for AV fiber extenders)

---

**Q9.** When installing digital signage displays in portrait orientation, what hardware consideration is essential?
- A) No special considerations
- B) Verify display is rated for portrait operation (thermal dissipation differs)
- C) Only LCD displays work in portrait
- D) Requires special software only

**Answer:** B) Verify display rated for portrait operation

---

**Q10.** For a 4K HDR video distribution system over Category cable, what minimum cable category is required?
- A) Cat5e
- B) Cat6
- C) Cat6A
- D) Depends on distance and manufacturer

**Answer:** D) Depends on distance/manufacturer; Cat6A recommended for reliability

## SECTION 3: COMPONENTS & FUNCTIONS (10 Questions)

### Entry Level

**Q1.** What is the function of an AV receiver (AVR)?
- A) Record video
- B) Switch and route audio/video sources, amplify audio for speakers
- C) Display images
- D) Connect to internet only

**Answer:** B) Switch/route sources and amplify audio

---

**Q2.** What does HDMI stand for?
- A) High Definition Media Interface
- B) High-Definition Multimedia Interface
- C) Home Digital Media Input
- D) High Density Monitor Input

**Answer:** B) High-Definition Multimedia Interface

---

**Q3.** What is the purpose of a video scaler?
- A) Make displays larger
- B) Convert video resolution from one format to another
- C) Add more inputs
- D) Improve audio quality

**Answer:** B) Convert video resolution between formats

---

### Intermediate Level

**Q4.** What is the function of EDID in an HDMI connection?
- A) Encrypt content
- B) Communicate display capabilities (resolution, audio formats) to source
- C) Extend cable length
- D) Convert digital to analog

**Answer:** B) Extended Display Identification Data - communicates display capabilities

---

**Q5.** What is the difference between an audio mixer and a DSP?
- A) No difference
- B) Mixer combines sources; DSP provides programmable signal processing
- C) Mixer is digital, DSP is analog
- D) DSP is for video only

**Answer:** B) Mixer combines; DSP adds programmable EQ, routing, delay, feedback suppression

---

**Q6.** What is HDCP and why does it sometimes cause "no signal" issues?
- A) Cable type
- B) High-bandwidth Digital Content Protection - handshake failures cause blank screen
- C) Resolution standard
- D) Audio format

**Answer:** B) Content protection; failed authentication = no picture

---

### Advanced Level

**Q7.** What is the function of a video wall processor vs. a simple splitter?
- A) No difference
- B) Processor scales, positions, and manages content across multiple displays; splitter duplicates
- C) Processor is wireless
- D) Splitter handles more displays

**Answer:** B) Processor manages content creatively; splitter just duplicates

---

**Q8.** What is "AEC" in audio conferencing systems?
- A) Automatic Equipment Control
- B) Acoustic Echo Cancellation - removes room echo from microphone signal
- C) Audio Enhancement Circuit
- D) Amplifier Efficiency Controller

**Answer:** B) Acoustic Echo Cancellation

---

**Q9.** What is the function of a "matrix switcher" in AV systems?
- A) Creates video effects
- B) Routes any input to any output independently
- C) Switches between analog and digital
- D) Matrices display content

**Answer:** B) Routes any input to any output simultaneously and independently

---

**Q10.** What is "lip sync delay" adjustment in AV systems?
- A) Microphone sensitivity
- B) Compensates for audio/video processing timing differences
- C) Display brightness
- D) Speaker volume

**Answer:** B) Adjusts audio timing to match video (processing delays create desync)

---

## SECTION 4: PROGRAMMING (10 Questions)

### Entry Level

**Q1.** What is the function of an AV control system (Crestron, Extron, AMX)?
- A) Improve video quality
- B) Automate and simplify operation of multiple AV components
- C) Increase volume
- D) Record meetings

**Answer:** B) Automate and centralize control of AV components

---

**Q2.** What communication protocol do most AV control systems use to control displays?
- A) HDMI
- B) RS-232 serial or IP
- C) Audio cables
- D) Infrared only

**Answer:** B) RS-232 serial or IP control

---

**Q3.** What is a "macro" in AV programming?
- A) Large display
- B) Sequence of commands executed with single button press
- C) Zoom level
- D) Recording feature

**Answer:** B) Sequence of commands executed together

---

### Intermediate Level

**Q4.** When programming a DSP for a conference room, what is "AEC reference signal"?
- A) Microphone input
- B) Far-end audio (from speakers) used to remove echo from microphones
- C) Room calibration tone
- D) Volume preset

**Answer:** B) Far-end audio used as reference for echo cancellation

---

**Q5.** What is "auto-switching" in a presentation room?
- A) Automatic power off
- B) System automatically selects active video input without user intervention
- C) Speaker selection
- D) Recording activation

**Answer:** B) Auto-selects active video input

---

**Q6.** What does "occupancy sensing" integration accomplish in AV programming?
- A) Counts people
- B) Activates AV system when room is occupied, powers down when empty
- C) Adjusts volume to crowd size
- D) Security function

**Answer:** B) Energy-saving auto on/off based on room occupancy

---

### Advanced Level

**Q7.** What is "Dante" in professional AV networking?
- A) Display calibration
- B) Audio-over-IP networking protocol for digital audio transport
- C) Video compression
- D) Control system protocol

**Answer:** B) Audio-over-IP networking standard (Audinate)

---

**Q8.** When programming room combining/dividing systems, what logic must be implemented?
- A) Simple on/off
- B) Partition sensor monitoring with automatic signal routing and control
- C) Volume adjustment only
- D) Display switching only

**Answer:** B) Partition status drives audio zones, display assignments, control boundaries

---

**Q9.** What is "USB-HID" pass-through in AV programming for video conferencing?
- A) Audio routing
- B) Passing USB Human Interface Device commands (touch, mouse, keyboard) over extension
- C) Video compression
- D) Display control

**Answer:** B) Extends USB peripheral control over AV signal path

---

**Q10.** When integrating AV with building management systems (BMS), what protocol is commonly used?
- A) HDMI-CEC
- B) BACnet or Modbus
- C) Dante
- D) HDCP

**Answer:** B) BACnet or Modbus for building automation integration
