## SECTION 1: CODES & STANDARDS (20 Questions)

### Entry Level

**Q1.** What organization publishes standards for professional AV system design and installation?
- A) NFPA
- B) AVIXA (formerly InfoComm)
- C) IEEE
- D) UL

**Answer:** B) AVIXA (formerly InfoComm)

---

**Q2.** What NEC article covers audio system wiring in commercial installations?
- A) NEC Article 640 (Audio Signal Processing Equipment)
- B) NEC Article 725
- C) NEC Article 800
- D) NEC Article 760

**Answer:** A) NEC Article 640

---

**Q3.** What does "CTS" certification stand for in the AV industry?
- A) Certified Technical Specialist
- B) Certified Technology Specialist
- C) Commercial Technical Standards
- D) Certified Television Systems

**Answer:** B) Certified Technology Specialist (AVIXA certification)

---

**Q4.** What does "AFF" stand for in AV installation mounting specifications?
- A) Audio Frequency Filter
- B) Above Finished Floor
- C) Amplifier Feed Forward
- D) Analog Full Frame

**Answer:** B) Above Finished Floor

---

**Q5.** What type of cable carries both audio and video signals in modern AV systems?
- A) Coaxial only
- B) HDMI
- C) Speaker wire
- D) Cat3

**Answer:** B) HDMI (High-Definition Multimedia Interface)

---

**Q6.** What NEC article governs Class 2 and Class 3 low-voltage circuits commonly used in AV control wiring?
- A) NEC Article 640
- B) NEC Article 725
- C) NEC Article 800
- D) NEC Article 770

**Answer:** B) NEC Article 725

### Entry Level

- B) 3.5mm TRS

**Q2.** When installing HDMI cables, what is the recommended maximum distance for passive cable?
- A) 10 feet
- B) 25 feet
- C) 50 feet
- D) 100 feet

**Answer:** C) 50 feet (15m) typical maximum for passive HDMI

---

**Q3.** What is the correct pin configuration for T568B Ethernet termination used in AV-over-IP?

- B) Orange-White, Orange, Green-White, Blue, Blue-White, Green, Brown-White, Brown
- C) Any order as long as both ends match
- D) Manufacturer specific

**Answer:** B) Orange-White, Orange, Green-White, Blue, Blue-White, Green, Brown-White, Brown

---

**Q4.** What is the difference between a balanced and unbalanced audio connection?
- A) Balanced is louder
- B) Balanced uses two signal conductors plus ground to reject noise; unbalanced uses one signal plus ground
- C) Unbalanced sounds better
- D) No practical difference

**Answer:** B) Balanced uses two conductors + ground for noise rejection; unbalanced uses one + ground

**Q5.** What type of cable is used to connect speakers to an amplifier in a low-impedance system?

**Q2.** What does HDMI stand for?

- B) High-Definition Multimedia Interface

- D) High Density Monitor Input

**Q3.** What is the purpose of a video scaler?

- B) Convert video resolution from one format to another

- D) Improve audio quality

**Answer:** B) Convert video resolution between formats

---

**Q4.** What does a "splitter" do in an AV system?
- A) Mixes audio channels
- B) Takes one input signal and duplicates it to multiple outputs
- C) Converts analog to digital
- D) Increases volume

**Q5.** What is an "amplifier" used for in an audio system?

- B) Increases the power of an audio signal to drive speakers
- C) Converts video formats

---

**Q6.** What is a "patch panel" in an AV installation?

**Q7.** What is the function of EDID in an HDMI connection?

**Q8.** What is the difference between an audio mixer and a DSP?

**Q12.** What is the purpose of a "power conditioner" or "UPS" in an AV equipment rack?

**Q1.** What is the function of an AV control system (Crestron, Extron, AMX)?
- A) Improve video quality
- B) Automate and simplify operation of multiple AV components
- C) Increase volume
- D) Record meetings

**Answer:** B) Automate and centralize control of AV components

---

**Q2.** What communication protocol do most AV control systems use to control displays?
- A) HDMI
- B) RS-232 serial or IP
- C) Audio cables
- D) Infrared only

**Answer:** B) RS-232 serial or IP control

---

**Q3.** What is a "macro" in AV programming?
- A) Large display
- B) Sequence of commands executed with single button press
- C) Zoom level
- D) Recording feature

**Answer:** B) Sequence of commands executed together

---

**Q4.** What does a "power on" command in an AV control system typically include?

**Q6.** What is a "touch panel" in AV control systems?
