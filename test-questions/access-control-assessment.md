## SECTION 1: CODES & STANDARDS (20 Questions)

### Entry Level

**Q1.** What is the primary UL standard that covers access control system equipment?
- A) UL 681
- B) UL 294
- C) UL 1076
- D) UL 2050

**Answer:** B) UL 294

---

**Q2.** According to NFPA 101, what must happen to magnetically-locked doors during a fire alarm?
- A) Remain locked for security
- B) Automatically release to allow egress
- C) Sound a local alarm
- D) Switch to card-only access

**Answer:** B) Automatically release to allow egress

---

**Q3.** What does ADA require regarding the mounting height of card readers?
- A) Maximum 36 inches AFF
- B) Maximum 48 inches AFF for forward approach
- C) Minimum 60 inches AFF
- D) No specific requirement

**Answer:** B) Maximum 48 inches AFF for forward approach

---

**Q4.** What does "egress" mean in fire and life safety code?
- A) Entry into a building
- B) The ability to exit a building freely
- C) Emergency lockdown
- D) Fire alarm activation

**Answer:** B) The ability to exit a building freely

---

**Q5.** What NFPA standard addresses the Life Safety Code for building egress?
- A) NFPA 70
- B) NFPA 72
- C) NFPA 101
- D) NFPA 25

**Answer:** C) NFPA 101 (Life Safety Code)

---

**Q6.** Per NEC, what low-voltage circuit class applies to most access control wiring?
- A) Class 1
- B) Class 2
- C) Class 3
- D) Power-limited fire alarm

**Answer:** B) Class 2 (per NEC Article 725)

---

- B) Levels A, B, C; Level A highest

**Q9.** According to NFPA 80, what is the maximum force allowed to open a fire door with access control?

- C) 30 lbf

**Q11.** What frequency do modern smart cards (MIFARE, DESFire) operate at?

- B) 13.56 MHz
- C) 900 MHz
- D) 433 MHz

**Answer:** B) 13.56 MHz

---

**Q12.** What is "Wiegand" in the context of access control?
- A) A lock brand
- B) A communication protocol between readers and controllers using separate data lines
- C) A card technology
- D) A door type

## SECTION 2: INSTALLATION (20 Questions)

### Entry Level

**Q1.** When installing a magnetic lock (maglock), what is the critical first step?
- A) Test the lock with power
- B) Verify door swing direction and frame material
- C) Install the armature plate
- D) Connect the wiring

**Answer:** B) Verify door swing direction and frame material

---

**Q2.** What is the standard wire gauge for powering a 600lb maglock with a 50-foot cable run?
- A) 22 AWG
- B) 18 AWG
- C) 16 AWG
- D) 14 AWG

**Answer:** B) 18 AWG

---

**Q3.** When installing a door contact (position sensor), where should the magnet be mounted?
- A) On the door frame (fixed)
- B) On the door itself (moving)
- C) Inside the access controller
- D) Above the door frame

**Answer:** B) On the door itself (moving)

---

**Q4.** What cable type is standard for Wiegand reader connections?
- A) Cat6
- B) Multi-conductor shielded 22 AWG (typically 6-8 conductors)
- C) Coaxial
- D) Speaker wire

**Answer:** B) Multi-conductor shielded 22 AWG cable

---

**Q5.** What is the typical power supply voltage for access control hardware?
- A) 120V AC
- B) 12V DC or 24V DC
- C) 48V DC
- D) 5V DC

**Answer:** B) 12V DC or 24V DC depending on hardware

---

**Q6.** When mounting a card reader outdoors, what weather protection rating should it have?
- A) No rating needed
- B) IP65 or higher for weather resistance
- C) UL listed only
- D) NEMA 1

**Answer:** B) IP65 or higher for outdoor weather protection

---

### Intermediate Level

**Q7.** When wiring an OSDP reader to a controller, what is the maximum cable distance?

---

**Q10.** What is the maximum Wiegand cable distance from reader to controller?
- A) 100 feet

---

**Q11.** When installing a power supply for access control, what battery backup is typically required?
- A) No battery needed
- B) Minimum 4 hours of standby battery backup
- C) 24 hours

**Answer:** B) Minimum 4 hours standby for UL-listed systems

---

**Q12.** What is proper installation practice for concealed door contacts on metal frames?

**Q13.** When installing a controller in a network closet, what grounding is required?

## SECTION 3: COMPONENTS & FUNCTIONS (20 Questions)

### Entry Level

**Q1.** What is the primary function of an access control "controller" or "panel"?
- A) Display access events
- B) Make access decisions and control door hardware
- C) Store employee photos
- D) Connect to internet

**Answer:** B) Make access decisions and control door hardware

---

**Q2.** What does a "Request to Exit" (REX) device do?
- A) Requests permission from security
- B) Signals the controller that someone is exiting to prevent false alarms
- C) Unlocks the door remotely
- D) Logs exit events

**Answer:** B) Signals controller to prevent false "door forced" alarms during exit

---

**Q3.** What is the difference between "fail-safe" and "fail-secure" locks?
- A) Fail-safe is more secure
- B) Fail-safe unlocks on power loss; fail-secure locks on power loss
- C) Fail-secure requires battery
- D) No difference

**Answer:** B) Fail-safe unlocks on power loss; fail-secure locks on power loss

---

**Q4.** What is a "credential" in access control?
- A) A password only
- B) Any item or data used to identify a person (card, fob, PIN, biometric)
- C) A controller brand
- D) A lock type

**Answer:** B) Any identification token: card, fob, PIN, biometric, mobile credential

---

**Q5.** What does a "door contact" or "door position switch" monitor?
- A) Lock status
- B) Whether the door is open or closed
- C) Card reader activity
- D) Temperature

**Answer:** B) Whether the door is physically open or closed

---

**Q6.** What is an "electric strike"?
- A) A magnet
- B) An electromechanical lock component that replaces a standard strike plate, allowing remote unlock
- C) A card reader
- D) A panic bar

**Answer:** B) Electromechanical device replacing standard strike plate for remote release

---

### Intermediate Level

**Q7.** In Wiegand protocol, what do "D0" and "D1" wires represent?
- A) Power and ground
- B) Data 0 (zero bit) and Data 1 (one bit) signal lines
- C) Door contact and REX
- D) Reader tamper and LED

**Answer:** B) Data 0 and Data 1 signal lines

---

**Q8.** What is the function of an EOL resistor in a door contact circuit?
- A) Limit current
- B) Provide supervision to detect cut or shorted wires
- C) Filter noise
- D) Enable wireless

**Answer:** B) Supervision to detect tampered/cut/shorted wires

---

**Q9.** What is the purpose of a "shunt timer" in an access controller?
- A) Delay lock activation
- B) Allow door to remain open without alarm for a set period after valid access
- C) Schedule automatic unlocking
- D) Control reader LEDs

**Answer:** B) Masks door contact temporarily after valid access

---

**Q10.** What is a "proximity" card and how does it work?
- A) A magnetic stripe card
- B) A contactless card containing a coil and chip that transmits its ID when energized by the reader's RF field
- C) A barcode card
- D) A biometric sensor

**Answer:** B) Contactless card with coil/chip that transmits ID via RF field

---

**Q11.** What is a "magnetic lock" (maglock) and how does it secure a door?
- A) A traditional keyed lock
- B) An electromagnet that holds a metal armature plate, creating a strong bond when energized
- C) A deadbolt with motor
- D) A card reader with built-in lock

**Answer:** B) Electromagnet holding armature plate when energized (fail-safe)

---

**Q12.** What is the standard holding force rating for commercial maglocks?
- A) 150 lbs
- B) 300 lbs
- C) 600 lbs or 1200 lbs
- D) 2000 lbs

**Answer:** C) 600 lbs (single) or 1200 lbs (double/shear) are standard commercial ratings

---

**Q13.** What is a "smart card" reader and how does it differ from a proximity reader?
- A) Same technology
- B) Smart cards use encrypted data storage and mutual authentication; proximity just broadcasts a fixed ID
- C) Proximity is more secure
- D) Smart cards are larger

**Answer:** B) Smart cards use encryption and authentication; proximity sends fixed unencrypted ID

---

### Advanced Level

**Q14.** What is "anti-passback" and what are the two main types?
- A) Preventing tailgating; physical and virtual
- B) Preventing credential sharing; hard and soft passback
- C) Preventing card cloning; hardware and software
- D) Preventing unauthorized exit

**Answer:** B) Preventing credential sharing; hard (deny) and soft (log violation)

---

**Q15.** In OSDP, what is the "secure channel" handshake and what encryption does it use?
- A) Encrypts card data; DES
- B) Establishes encrypted reader-controller communication; AES-128
- C) Verifies card; RSA
- D) Protects stored data; SHA-256

**Answer:** B) Encrypted reader-controller communication via AES-128

---

**Q16.** What is a "dual-technology" reader and when is it used?

- D) Primary and backup

---

**Q17.** What does "two-person rule" enforcement require in access control?

- B) Guard and badge

---

**Q18.** What is "mobile credential" technology?

- C) A mobile app for monitoring only

---

**Q19.** What is a "controller board" vs. a "reader interface module"?

**Q20.** What is "biometric" access control and what are common modalities?

## SECTION 4: PROGRAMMING & CONFIGURATION (20 Questions)

### Entry Level

**Q1.** What are "access levels" in access control programming?
- A) Physical floors
- B) Groups of doors and time schedules assigned to cardholders
- C) Security clearances
- D) Reader mounting heights

**Answer:** B) Groups of doors and time schedules assigned to cardholders

---

**Q2.** What is a "time schedule" in access control programming?
- A) System clock setting
- B) Defined time periods during which access rules apply
- C) Daylight saving adjustment
- D) Event log timestamps

**Answer:** B) Defined time periods when access rules apply

---

**Q3.** When programming a new cardholder, what minimum information is required?
- A) Just the card number
- B) Name, card number, and at least one access level
- C) Photo, fingerprint, badge design
- D) Department, salary, emergency contact

**Answer:** B) Name, card number, and at least one access level

---

**Q4.** What is an "access group" or "clearance level"?
- A) A user's department
- B) A defined set of doors and schedules that can be assigned to multiple cardholders
- C) A physical area
- D) An alarm zone

**Answer:** B) A set of doors and schedules assignable to cardholders

---

**Q5.** What is a "holiday schedule" in access control?
- A) Vacation tracking
- B) Special dates where normal access schedules are overridden (e.g., locked all day)
- C) Pay schedule
- D) Maintenance window

**Answer:** B) Dates where normal schedules are overridden

---

**Q6.** What does "access granted" vs. "access denied" indicate in an event log?
- A) Door status
- B) Whether the presented credential had valid permissions at that door and time
- C) System online status
- D) Reader battery level

**Answer:** B) Whether the credential had valid permissions for that door at that time

---

### Intermediate Level

**Q7.** What is "global anti-passback" vs. "local anti-passback"?
- A) Global works across all doors; local works per door or area
- B) Global uses GPS; local uses reader
- C) Global requires internet; local offline
- D) Global is optional; local mandatory

**Answer:** A) Global tracks across all doors; local per door/area

---

**Q8.** What is the difference between "unlock time" and "held open time"?
- A) No difference
- B) Unlock time = lock release duration; held open time = max before alarm
- C) Unlock = schedule; held open = override

---

**Q9.** How do you program elevator floor control?
- A) Assign cab reader to floor access levels
- B) Program floor buttons as individual "doors" with access levels
- C) Time schedules only

---

**Q10.** What is "auto-unlock" schedule programming?
- A) Automatic firmware updates
- B) Doors automatically unlock during business hours based on time schedule

**Q11.** What is the purpose of "alarm masking" in access control programming?
