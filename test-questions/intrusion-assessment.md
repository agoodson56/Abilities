## SECTION 1: CODES & STANDARDS (20 Questions)

### Entry Level

**Q1.** What is the primary UL standard covering intrusion detection and alarm systems?
- A) UL 294
- B) UL 681
- C) UL 1076
- D) UL 2050

**Answer:** B) UL 681

---

**Q2.** What does "UL Listed" mean for an intrusion detection device?
- A) The device is the most expensive
- B) The device has been tested and meets UL safety/performance standards
- C) The device is made in the USA
- D) The device is waterproof

**Answer:** B) Tested and meets UL safety/performance standards

---

**Q3.** According to UL standards, what is the minimum battery backup duration for a residential burglar alarm?
- A) 4 hours
- B) 12 hours
- C) 24 hours
- D) 48 hours

**Answer:** C) 24 hours

**Q4.** What does "ANSI" stand for in the context of alarm standards?

- B) American National Standards Institute
- C) Alarm Network Standards Interface
- D) Association of National Security Inspectors

**Answer:** B) American National Standards Institute

---

**Q5.** What is NFPA 731 and how does it relate to intrusion systems?
- A) Fire alarm standard

- C) Electrical code
- D) Building code

**Answer:** B) Standard for installation of electronic premises security systems

---

**Q6.** What NEC article governs low-voltage alarm circuit wiring?

- C) NEC Article 800
- D) NEC Article 820

**Answer:** B) NEC Article 725 (Class 2 circuits)

- B) On the moving door
- C) Inside the panel
- D) On the adjacent wall

**Answer:** B) On the moving door

---

**Q2.** What is the typical wire gauge for hardwired zone wiring in residential systems?
- A) 14 AWG
- B) 18 AWG
- C) 22 AWG
- D) 26 AWG

**Answer:** C) 22 AWG

---

**Q3.** What is the recommended maximum mounting height for a standard PIR detector?
- A) 6 feet
- B) 7-8 feet
- C) 10-12 feet
- D) 15 feet

**Answer:** B) 7-8 feet

**Q4.** Where should the alarm control panel be installed?

**Q7.** Why should PIR detectors avoid facing windows?

**Q8.** What is DEOL resistor wiring configuration?

**Q10.** When installing a keypad, what is the recommended mounting height?

## SECTION 3: COMPONENTS & FUNCTIONS (20 Questions)

### Entry Level

**Q1.** What does PIR stand for, and what does it detect?
- A) Passive Infrared; changes in heat radiation
- B) Powered Infrared Receiver; infrared light
- C) Proximity Indicator Relay; objects
- D) Perimeter Intrusion Responder; movement

**Answer:** A) Passive Infrared; changes in heat radiation

---

**Q2.** What is a "zone" in an intrusion system?
- A) A room
- B) An input circuit monitoring one or more sensors
- C) The outdoor perimeter
- D) A time schedule

**Answer:** B) An input circuit monitoring one or more sensors

---

**Q3.** What is the purpose of a keypad/arming station?
- A) Detect motion
- B) Provide user interface for arming/disarming
- C) Power sensors
- D) Send signals to monitoring

**Answer:** B) User interface for arming/disarming

---

**Q4.** What is a "door/window contact" sensor?
- A) A motion detector
- B) A magnetic switch that detects when a door or window is opened
- C) A glass break detector
- D) A camera

## SECTION 4: PROGRAMMING (20 Questions)

### Entry Level

**Q1.** What is entry delay timing typically set to?
- A) Before arming
- B) 30-45 seconds after entering before alarm
- C) Before sirens
- D) Monitoring station wait time

**Answer:** B) 30-45 seconds after entering to allow disarming

---

**Q2.** What is "stay" or "perimeter" arming mode?
- A) Arms all zones
- B) Arms perimeter (doors/windows) only, disables interior motion
- C) Temporary arming
- D) Arms without delays

**Answer:** B) Arms perimeter only, disables interior motion

---

**Q3.** Why use clear zone descriptions?
- A) Required by code
- B) Helps responders identify alarm locations quickly
- C) Reduces programming time
- D) Required for wireless

**Answer:** B) Helps responders identify alarm locations

---

**Q4.** What is "away" or "full" arming mode?
- A) Same as stay mode
- B) Arms all zones including interior motion detectors; building should be fully unoccupied
- C) Disarms the system
- D) Test mode

**Answer:** B) Arms all zones; building should be unoccupied

---

**Q5.** What is "exit delay" and how long is it typically set?
- A) Time to enter before alarm
- B) Time allowed to leave after arming before system becomes active; typically 45-60 seconds
- C) Time before battery dies
- D) Monitoring response time

**Answer:** B) Time to leave after arming; typically 45-60 seconds

---

**Q6.** What is a "master code" vs. a "user code" on an alarm panel?
- A) Same function
- B) Master code has full programming access; user codes can arm/disarm with limited functions
- C) Master is for monitoring only
- D) User code is more powerful

**Answer:** B) Master has full access; user codes have limited permissions

---

**Q7.** What is cross-zone verification?
- A) Requires two zones to trip in time window before dispatch

---

**Q8.** What is a 24-hour zone?
- A) Only active daytime
- B) Always active regardless of arm status; used for panic, tamper, environmental sensors
- C) Active when occupied
- D) After 24 hours inactivity

**Answer:** B) Always active regardless of arm status

---

**Q9.** What is a "report code" for central station?
- A) Account number
- B) Numeric code identifying specific event type (alarm, trouble, open/close)
- C) Password
- D) Area code

**Answer:** B) Code identifying specific event type

---

**Q10.** What is "Contact ID" communication format?
- A) Customer phone number
- B) Industry-standard digital alarm reporting format (SIA DC-05) used between panel and central station
- C) Installer contact information

**Answer:** B) Standard digital alarm reporting format (SIA DC-05)

**Q11.** What is "auto arm" programming?

- B) Automatically arms the system at a preset time if not already armed (e.g., 11 PM nightly)

**Q14.** What is swinger shutdown programming?
