## TotalMaster ELV Academy - Entry Level to Advanced Journeyman

## SECTION 1: CODES & STANDARDS (10 Questions)

### Entry Level

**Q1.** What is the primary UL standard that covers intrusion detection and alarm systems?
- A) UL 294
- B) UL 681
- C) UL 1076
- D) UL 2050

**Answer:** B) UL 681
> *Reference: UL 681 - Installation and Classification of Burglar and Holdup Alarm Systems*

---

**Q2.** What does "UL Listed" mean for an intrusion detection device?
- A) The device is the most expensive option
- B) The device has been tested and meets UL safety/performance standards
- C) The device is made in the USA
- D) The device is waterproof

**Answer:** B) The device has been tested and meets UL safety/performance standards

---

**Q3.** According to UL standards, what is the minimum battery backup duration required for a residential burglar alarm?
- A) 4 hours
- B) 12 hours
- C) 24 hours
- D) 48 hours

**Answer:** C) 24 hours

---

### Intermediate Level

**Q4.** What are the UL alarm system classification grades, and which provides the HIGHEST protection?
- A) Grade D is highest
- B) Grade A is highest
- C) Grade AA is highest
- D) All grades are equal

**Answer:** C) Grade AA is highest

---

**Q5.** According to NEC Article 760, what class of circuit is a burglar alarm signaling circuit under 150V?
- A) Class 1
- B) Class 2
- C) Power-limited fire alarm (PLFA)
- D) Class 2 per NEC 725

**Answer:** D) Class 2 per NEC 725

---

**Q6.** What ANSI/SIA standard defines false alarm reduction features like "Cross-Zone"?
- A) ANSI/SIA CP-01-2019
- B) UL 681
- C) NFPA 731
- D) IEEE 802.3

**Answer:** A) ANSI/SIA CP-01-2019

---

### Advanced Level

**Q7.** Under SIA CP-01, what is the minimum "Cross-Zone" verification time window?
- A) 30 seconds; reduces false alarms from single-zone activations
- B) 60 seconds
- C) 120 seconds
- D) No minimum

**Answer:** A) 30 seconds minimum

---

**Q8.** What European standard (EN 50131) grade is similar to UL Grade A commercial systems?
- A) EN Grade 1
- B) EN Grade 2
- C) EN Grade 3
- D) EN Grade 4

**Answer:** C) EN Grade 3

---

**Q9.** According to NFPA 731, what determines response time for monitoring station dispatch?
- A) Immediate (0 seconds)
- B) 60 seconds maximum
- C) 180 seconds maximum
- D) Per subscriber contract

**Answer:** D) Per subscriber contract

---

**Q10.** What documentation must be provided after UL-certificated commercial installation?
- A) Verbal confirmation only
- B) Certificate of Installation with panel serial number and grade classification
- C) Standardized invoice
- D) Building permit only

**Answer:** B) Certificate of Installation with panel serial number and grade classification

- C) Inside the panel

- C) 10-12 feet
- D) 15 feet

**Q4.** Why should PIR detectors avoid facing windows?

## SECTION 3: COMPONENTS & FUNCTIONS (10 Questions)

### Entry Level

**Q1.** What does PIR stand for, and what does it detect?
- A) Passive Infrared; changes in heat radiation
- B) Powered Infrared Receiver; infrared light
- C) Proximity Indicator Relay; nearby objects
- D) Perimeter Intrusion Responder; outdoor movement

**Answer:** A) Passive Infrared; changes in heat radiation

---

**Q2.** What is a "zone" in an intrusion system?
- A) A room
- B) An input circuit monitoring one or more sensors
- C) The outdoor perimeter
- D) A time schedule

**Answer:** B) An input circuit monitoring one or more sensors

---

**Q3.** What is the purpose of a keypad/arming station?
- A) Detect motion
- B) Provide user interface for arming/disarming
- C) Power sensors
- D) Send signals to monitoring

**Answer:** B) Provide user interface for arming/disarming

---

### Intermediate Level

**Q4.** What is a dual-technology motion detector?
- A) Detects two directions
- B) Combines PIR and microwave; reduces false alarms
- C) Works day and night
- D) Wireless and hardwired

**Answer:** B) Combines PIR and microwave; reduces false alarms

---

**Q5.** What is the function of an EOL resistor?
- A) Limit current
- B) Provide zone supervision to detect tampering
- C) Reduce voltage
- D) Filter noise

**Answer:** B) Provide zone supervision to detect tampering

---

**Q6.** What is the difference between instant and delayed zones?

- B) Instant triggers immediately; delayed allows entry time
- C) When armed status
- D) Wired vs wireless

**Answer:** B) Instant triggers immediately; delayed allows entry time

---

## SECTION 4: PROGRAMMING (10 Questions)

### Entry Level

**Q1.** What is entry delay timing typically set to?
- A) Before arming
- B) 30-45 seconds after entering before alarm
- C) Before sirens
- D) Monitoring station wait time

**Answer:** B) 30-45 seconds after entering before alarm

---

**Q2.** What is "stay" or "perimeter" arming mode?
- A) Arms all zones
- B) Arms perimeter only, disables interior motion
- C) Temporary arming
- D) Arms without delays

**Answer:** B) Arms perimeter only, disables interior motion

---

**Q3.** Why use clear zone descriptions?
- A) Required by code
- B) Helps responders identify alarm locations
- C) Reduces programming time
- D) Required for wireless

**Answer:** B) Helps responders identify alarm locations

---

### Intermediate Level

**Q4.** What is cross-zone verification?
- A) Requires two zones trip in time window before dispatch
- B) Links zones on opposite sides
- C) Duplicates zone programming
- D) Disables adjacent zones

**Answer:** A) Requires two zones trip in time window before dispatch

---

**Q5.** What is a 24-hour zone?
- A) Only active daytime
- B) Always active regardless of arm status; panic, tamper, environmental
- C) Active when occupied
- D) After 24 hours inactivity

**Answer:** B) Always active regardless of arm status

---

**Q6.** What is a "report code" for central station?
- A) Account number
- B) Numeric code identifying specific event type
- C) Password
- D) Area code

**Answer:** B) Numeric code identifying specific event type

---

### Advanced Level

**Q7.** What is swinger shutdown programming?
- A) Disables zone after repeated trips; typically 2-3
- B) Shuts down keypad
- C) Disables siren
- D) Closes partition

**Answer:** A) Disables zone after repeated trips; typically 2-3

---

**Q8.** What must be met for "secure arming" with access control?
- A) Battery charged
- B) All access doors must be secured (closed and locked)
- C) All users logged out
- D) Monitoring online

**Answer:** B) All access doors must be secured (closed and locked)

---

**Q9.** What is "recent closing" programming?
- A) Cancels arming
- B) If alarm shortly after arming, may delay dispatch for verification
- C) Cancels subscription
- D) Closes all zones

**Answer:** B) If alarm shortly after arming, may delay dispatch for verification

---

**Q10.** What feature enables CCTV recording, door locking, and text on intrusion?
- A) Zone programming
- B) User code programming
- C) Output/relay automation and event-triggered actions
- D) Telephone programming

**Answer:** C) Output/relay automation and event-triggered actions
