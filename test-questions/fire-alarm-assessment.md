## SECTION 1: CODES & STANDARDS (20 Questions)

### Entry Level

**Q1.** What is the primary NFPA standard governing fire alarm system installation?
- A) NFPA 70
- B) NFPA 72
- C) NFPA 101
- D) NFPA 25

**Answer:** B) NFPA 72 - National Fire Alarm and Signaling Code

---

**Q2.** What does "AHJ" stand for in fire alarm codes?
- A) Automatic Heat Junction
- B) Authority Having Jurisdiction
- C) Alarm Hardware Junction
- D) Approved Hardware Justification

**Answer:** B) Authority Having Jurisdiction

---

**Q3.** According to NFPA 72, smoke detectors must be tested at what minimum interval?
- A) Weekly
- B) Monthly
- C) Annually
- D) Every 5 years

**Answer:** C) Annually

---

**Q4.** What does NFPA stand for?
- A) National Fire Protection Agency
- B) National Fire Protection Association
- C) National Firefighter Professional Authority
- D) North American Fire Prevention Act

**Answer:** B) National Fire Protection Association

---

**Q5.** What NEC article covers fire alarm system wiring?
- A) NEC Article 725
- B) NEC Article 760
- C) NEC Article 800
- D) NEC Article 820

**Answer:** B) NEC Article 760 (Fire Alarm Systems)

---

**Q6.** What organization provides "listing" and "labeling" of fire alarm equipment in the US?
- A) NFPA
- B) UL (Underwriters Laboratories)
- C) ICC
- D) OSHA

**Answer:** B) UL (Underwriters Laboratories)

---

**Q8.** According to NFPA 72, what is the required sound level for audible notification appliances in sleeping areas?

**Q10.** What is the minimum required sound level for audible notification appliances in non-sleeping commercial areas per NFPA 72?

- B) 75 dBA
- C) 85 dBA
- D) 90 dBA

**Answer:** A) 60 dBA or 15 dBA above average ambient, whichever is greater

**Q11.** Per NFPA 72, how often must fire alarm batteries be tested under load?
- A) Monthly

- C) Annually

**Answer:** C) Annually (visual inspection semi-annually)

**Q12.** What UL standard applies to fire alarm control panels (FACPs)?

**Q15.** According to NFPA 72 Chapter 29, what is the maximum allowable total pathway survivability time for critical circuit supervision?

**Q17.** What NFPA standard covers life safety (including egress and notification requirements)?
- A) NFPA 72

- C) NFPA 70

---

**Q18.** Per NFPA 72, what is the maximum spacing between visual notification appliances in a corridor?

### Entry Level

**Q1.** What is the minimum distance a smoke detector must be from an air supply diffuser?
- A) 12 inches
- B) 36 inches
- C) 48 inches
- D) 6 feet

**Answer:** B) 36 inches (3 feet per NFPA 72)

---

**Q2.** What color wiring is typically used for fire alarm circuits?
- A) Black and white
- B) Red
- C) Blue
- D) Green

**Answer:** B) Red (industry standard for fire alarm wiring)

---

**Q3.** What is the ADA-required mounting height for fire alarm pull stations?
- A) 36-42 inches AFF
- B) 42-48 inches AFF
- C) 48-54 inches AFF
- D) 54-60 inches AFF

**Answer:** B) 42-48 inches AFF

---

**Q4.** Where should a manual pull station be installed relative to an exit door?
- A) Above the door
- B) Within 5 feet of the exit on the egress side
- C) In the nearest room
- D) At least 10 feet from the exit

**Answer:** B) Within 5 feet of the exit on the egress side per NFPA 72

---

**Q5.** What is the correct placement of a smoke detector on a smooth ceiling?
- A) Against the wall
- B) Not less than 4 inches from any wall (dead air space)
- C) Only in the center of the room
- D) At any location on the ceiling

**Answer:** B) Not less than 4 inches from any wall

---

**Q6.** What type of cable is required for fire alarm circuits in non-plenum areas?
- A) Standard Romex
- B) FPLR or FPLP rated fire alarm cable
- C) Cat6 data cable

**Answer:** B) FPLR (riser) or FPLP (plenum) rated fire alarm cable per NEC 760

**Q8.** What supervision is required for fire alarm circuit wiring per NEC Article 760?

**Q10.** What is the proper wire type and gauge for SLC (Signaling Line Circuit) loops?

**Q11.** What is the required strobe candela for a wall-mounted appliance in a 20×20 room per NFPA 72?

**Q12.** When installing heat detectors, what clearance is required from HVAC diffusers?

**Q13.** What is required at the end of a Class B NAC circuit?

## SECTION 3: COMPONENTS & FUNCTIONS (20 Questions)

### Entry Level

**Q1.** What is the function of a Fire Alarm Control Panel (FACP)?
- A) Extinguish fires
- B) Monitor initiating devices and activate notification appliances
- C) Store water for sprinklers
- D) Cool electrical equipment

**Answer:** B) Monitor initiating devices and activate notification appliances

---

**Q2.** What is the difference between a smoke detector and a smoke alarm?
- A) No difference
- B) Detector connects to panel; alarm is standalone with built-in sounder
- C) Detector is larger
- D) Alarm is commercial grade

**Answer:** B) Detector connects to panel; alarm is standalone with built-in sounder

---

**Q3.** What does a manual pull station do?
- A) Automatically detects smoke
- B) Allows human-initiated fire alarm activation
- C) Opens sprinkler valves
- D) Calls fire department directly

**Answer:** B) Allows human-initiated fire alarm activation

---

**Q4.** What is a "horn/strobe" notification appliance?
- A) A detection device
- B) A combination audible horn and visual strobe light that alerts occupants
- C) A control panel accessory
- D) A sensor

**Answer:** B) A combination audible and visual notification device

---

**Q5.** What does an EOL (End of Line) resistor do in a fire alarm circuit?
- A) Powers the devices
- B) Provides circuit supervision so the panel can detect wiring faults
- C) Increases volume
- D) Filters noise

**Answer:** B) Provides circuit supervision to detect open or shorted wires

---

**Q6.** What is a heat detector and when is it used instead of a smoke detector?
- A) Same as smoke detector
- B) Detects temperature rise; used in kitchens, garages, dusty areas where smoke detectors would false alarm
- C) Only for outdoors
- D) More sensitive than smoke detectors

**Answer:** B) Detects temperature; used where smoke detectors would false alarm

**Q13.** What is an "annunciator" in a fire alarm system?

## SECTION 4: PROGRAMMING (20 Questions)

### Entry Level

**Q1.** What is "Cause and Effect" programming in fire alarm systems?
- A) Troubleshooting procedure
- B) Defines what outputs activate when specific inputs trigger
- C) Panel reset sequence
- D) Inspection documentation

**Answer:** B) Defines what outputs activate when specific inputs trigger

---

**Q2.** What is the purpose of programming detector sensitivity levels?
- A) Adjust volume
- B) Set detection threshold to environment (dusty, clean, humid)
- C) Change LED brightness
- D) Determine battery life

**Answer:** B) Set detection threshold appropriate for environment

---

**Q3.** What is a "zone" in fire alarm programming?
- A) A floor of the building
- B) A logical grouping of devices in a specific area for identification
- C) The panel enclosure
- D) Battery backup

**Answer:** B) A logical grouping of devices in a specific area

---

**Q4.** What happens when you program a device as "disabled" on a fire alarm panel?
- A) The device is physically removed
- B) The panel ignores signals from that device; it shows as disabled in system status
- C) The entire system shuts down
- D) Nothing changes

**Answer:** B) Panel ignores that device; shown as disabled status

---

**Q5.** What is the purpose of testing after programming changes?
- A) Optional good practice
- B) Required to verify all cause-and-effect sequences operate correctly before returning system to service
- C) Only needed for new installations
- D) Just for documentation

**Answer:** B) Required to verify programming works correctly before returning to service

---

**Q6.** What information does an addressable device's "address" provide to the panel?
- A) The device's price
- B) A unique identification number that tells the panel exactly which device is reporting
- C) The wire gauge
- D) Installation date

**Answer:** B) Unique ID so the panel knows which specific device is reporting

---

### Intermediate Level

**Q7.** What programming creates a "cross-zone" verification sequence?
- A) Two zones in different buildings
- B) Requires two zones to alarm within time window before notification or suppression
- C) Zones on opposite walls
- D) Duplicate zone programming

**Answer:** B) Requires two zones to alarm within time window before notification

---

**Q8.** What is "positive alarm sequence" (PAS) per NFPA 72?
- A) Confirming real fire
- B) Allows 15-second investigation period before automatic notification
- C) Alarm goes positive voltage
- D) Increasing alarm volume

**Answer:** B) 15-second initial period, 180-second investigation, auto-alarm if not reset

---

**Q9.** When programming elevator recall, what initiating devices trigger Phase I recall?
- A) All smoke detectors
- B) Lobby, machine room, and hoistway smoke detectors per ASME A17.1
- C) Pull stations only
- D) Heat detectors only

**Answer:** B) Lobby, machine room, and hoistway smoke detectors

---

**Q10.** What is "walk test mode" in fire alarm programming?
- A) Testing battery during a walk
- B) A test mode that activates devices briefly when triggered, allowing sequential device verification
- C) A daily self-test
- D) Panel reset procedure

**Answer:** B) Sequential device verification mode with brief activation

---

**Q11.** What is the purpose of "auto-sensitivity" programming on addressable detectors?
- A) Saves battery
- B) Automatically adjusts detector sensitivity based on environmental conditions and contamination
- C) Changes with seasons
- D) Reduces false alarms only

**Answer:** B) Automatic environmental sensitivity adjustment

---

**Q12.** When programming door holder release, what input triggers the release?
- A) Manual button only
- B) Any fire alarm signal (general alarm or area-specific) causes magnetic door holders to release
- C) Schedule-based only
- D) Security system only

**Answer:** B) Fire alarm signals cause door holders to release for fire barrier closure

---

**Q13.** What is "pre-signal" or "pre-alarm" programming?
- A) Testing before alarm
- B) Alert signal sent to trained staff before general evacuation, allowing investigation
- C) Equipment warm-up
- D) Battery pre-check

**Answer:** B) Alert to trained staff before general notification, allowing investigation

---

**Q19.** What is the programming requirement for "two-way firefighter telephone" systems?

**Q20.** When programming a fire alarm system with sprinkler supervision, what inputs must be monitored?
