## SECTION 1: CODES & STANDARDS (10 Questions)

### Entry Level

**Q1.** What is the primary NFPA standard governing fire alarm system installation?
- A) NFPA 70
- B) NFPA 72
- C) NFPA 101
- D) NFPA 25

**Answer:** B) NFPA 72 - National Fire Alarm and Signaling Code

---

**Q2.** What does "AHJ" stand for in fire alarm codes?
- A) Automatic Heatジャンクション
- B) Authority Having Jurisdiction
- C) Alarm Hardware Junction
- D) Approved Hardware Justification

**Answer:** B) Authority Having Jurisdiction

---

**Q3.** According to NFPA 72, smoke detectors must be tested at what minimum interval?
- A) Weekly
- B) Monthly
- C) Annually
- D) Every 5 years

**Answer:** C) Annually

---

### Intermediate Level

**Q4.** What is the maximum ceiling height for standard spot-type smoke detectors per NFPA 72?
- A) 10 feet
- B) 20 feet
- C) 30 feet
- D) No maximum

**Answer:** C) 30 feet

---

**Q5.** According to NFPA 72, what is the required sound level for audible notification appliances in sleeping areas?
- A) 65 dB
- B) 75 dB at pillow
- C) 85 dB
- D) 90 dB

**Answer:** B) 75 dB at pillow level

---

**Q6.** What is the maximum distance a point-type smoke detector can be from any wall in a smooth ceiling per NFPA 72?
- A) 15 feet
- B) 21 feet
- C) 30 feet
- D) 50 feet

**Answer:** B) 21 feet (half the listed spacing of 30ft for smooth ceilings)

---

### Advanced Level

**Q7.** Per NFPA 72, what defines a Class A initiating device circuit vs. Class B?
- A) Wire gauge
- B) Fault tolerance - Class A continues operation with single open
- C) Panel type
- D) Device quantity

**Answer:** B) Class A continues operation with single open fault

---

**Q8.** What UL standard applies to single and multiple station smoke alarms?
- A) UL 268
- B) UL 217
- C) UL 864
- D) UL 1971

**Answer:** B) UL 217 (UL 268 is for system smoke detectors)

---

**Q9.** According to NFPA 72 Chapter 29, what is the maximum allowable total pathway survivability time for critical circuit supervision?
- A) 4 hours
- B) 8 hours
- C) 24 hours
- D) 2 hours

**Answer:** D) 2 hours for Level 2 pathway survivability

---

**Q10.** What FM Global standard governs fire alarm equipment approval for industrial/commercial applications?
- A) FM 3210
- B) FM 3260
- C) FM 3010
- D) FM Approval Standard 3011

**Answer:** A) FM 3210 - Heat Detectors; FM 3230 - Smoke Detectors

## SECTION 2: INSTALLATION (10 Questions)

### Entry Level

**Q1.** What is the minimum distance a smoke detector must be from an air supply diffuser?
- A) 12 inches
- B) 36 inches
- C) 48 inches
- D) 6 feet

**Answer:** B) 36 inches (3 feet per NFPA 72)

---

**Q2.** What color wiring is typically used for fire alarm circuits?
- A) Black and white
- B) Red
- C) Blue
- D) Green

**Answer:** B) Red (industry standard for fire alarm wiring)

---

**Q3.** What is the ADA-required mounting height for fire alarm pull stations?
- A) 36-42 inches AFF
- B) 42-48 inches AFF
- C) 48-54 inches AFF
- D) 54-60 inches AFF

**Answer:** B) 42-48 inches AFF (operable part between 15" min and 48" max)

---

### Intermediate Level

**Q4.** When installing notification appliances, what is the required candela rating for a 50x50 room per NFPA 72 Table 18.5.5.5.1?
- A) 15 cd
- B) 30 cd
- C) 60 cd
- D) 110 cd

**Answer:** C) 60 cd for ceiling mount in 50x50 space

---

**Q5.** What supervision is required for fire alarm circuit wiring per NEC Article 760?
- A) No supervision required
- B) End-of-line device for opens and grounds
- C) Continuous monitoring at panel
- D) Both B and C

**Answer:** D) Both end-of-line supervision and panel monitoring

---

**Q6.** When installing duct smoke detectors, where must the sampling tubes extend?
- A) Outside the duct
- B) Across minimum 2/3 of duct width
- C) Full duct width with inlet in airstream
- D) Only at duct edges

**Answer:** C) Full duct width with inlet tubes positioned in airstream

---

### Advanced Level

**Q7.** What is the maximum wire run for #18 AWG on a Class B NAC circuit with 2A total load at 24VDC, allowing 10% voltage drop?
- A) 200 feet
- B) 350 feet
- C) 500 feet
- D) Calculate using V=IR formula

**Answer:** D) Must calculate: 24V × 10% = 2.4V drop allowed; using resistance tables

---

**Q8.** When installing a fire alarm system with pathway survivability Level 2, what physical protection is required?
- A) No protection needed
- B) 2-hour fire-rated enclosure or circuit integrity cable
- C) Metal conduit only
- D) Underground installation

**Answer:** B) 2-hour fire-rated enclosure or CI/MI cable

---

**Q9.** What additional requirement exists when installing smoke detectors in spaces with ceilings over 10 feet?
- A) No additional requirements
- B) Increase spacing proportionally
- C) Consider stratification and may require projected beam or air sampling
- D) Only heat detectors allowed

**Answer:** C) Consider stratification; may need alternative detection methods

---

**Q10.** During installation of an addressable SLC loop, what is the maximum number of devices typically supported per loop?
- A) 99 devices
- B) 127 devices
- C) 159 devices
- D) Manufacturer specific (often 127-255)

**Answer:** D) Manufacturer specific; commonly 127-255 addresses per loop

## SECTION 3: COMPONENTS & FUNCTIONS (10 Questions)

### Entry Level

**Q1.** What is the function of a Fire Alarm Control Panel (FACP)?
- A) Extinguish fires
- B) Monitor initiating devices and activate notification appliances
- C) Store water for sprinklers
- D) Cool electrical equipment

**Answer:** B) Monitor initiating devices and activate notification appliances

---

**Q2.** What is the difference between a smoke detector and a smoke alarm?
- A) No difference
- B) Detector connects to panel; alarm is standalone with built-in sounder
- C) Detector is larger
- D) Alarm is commercial grade

**Answer:** B) Detector connects to panel; alarm is standalone with built-in sounder

---

**Q3.** What does a manual pull station do?
- A) Automatically detects smoke
- B) Allows human-initiated fire alarm activation
- C) Opens sprinkler valves
- D) Calls fire department directly

**Answer:** B) Allows human-initiated fire alarm activation

---

### Intermediate Level

**Q4.** What is the difference between photoelectric and ionization smoke detection?
- A) No difference
- B) Photoelectric uses light; ionization uses radioactive particles
- C) Ionization is newer technology
- D) Photoelectric is for commercial only

**Answer:** B) Photoelectric uses light scattering; ionization uses radioactive source

---

**Q5.** What is a "supervisory" signal on a fire alarm system?
- A) Same as alarm
- B) Indicates abnormal condition requiring attention but not fire (valve closed, low pressure)
- C) System test mode
- D) Power failure

**Answer:** B) Indicates abnormal condition requiring attention but not fire

---

**Q6.** What is the function of an addressable monitor module?
- A) Display system status
- B) Monitor conventional zone or contact input and report address to panel
- C) Power notification appliances
- D) Supervise sprinkler flow

**Answer:** B) Monitor conventional zone or contact input and report address to panel

---

### Advanced Level

**Q7.** What is the function of a "releasing panel" vs. a standard FACP?
- A) No difference
- B) Controls fire suppression agent release (clean agent, pre-action, deluge)
- C) Releases doors only
- D) Standard panel with more zones

**Answer:** B) Controls fire suppression agent release

---

**Q8.** What is "alarm verification" on a fire alarm panel?
- A) Confirming alarm with fire department
- B) Panel resets detector and waits for second activation within time window
- C) Visual inspection by guard
- D) Monthly testing procedure

**Answer:** B) Panel resets detector and waits for second activation within time window

---

**Q9.** What is the function of a "Class A Riser" in fire alarm riser diagrams?
- A) Main power feed
- B) Return pathway for fault-tolerant loop wiring
- C) Telephone line
- D) Ground connection

**Answer:** B) Return pathway for fault-tolerant loop wiring

---

**Q10.** What is a "mass notification system" (MNS) and how does it differ from standard fire alarm?
- A) Louder notification
- B) Provides intelligible voice messaging for multiple emergency types, not just fire
- C) Notifies fire department faster
- D) Commercial fire alarm

**Answer:** B) Provides intelligible voice messaging for multiple emergency types

## SECTION 4: PROGRAMMING (10 Questions)

### Entry Level

**Q1.** What is "Cause and Effect" programming in fire alarm systems?
- A) Troubleshooting procedure
- B) Defines what outputs activate when specific inputs trigger (e.g., Zone 1 alarm activates NAC 1)
- C) Panel reset sequence
- D) Inspection documentation

**Answer:** B) Defines what outputs activate when specific inputs trigger

---

**Q2.** What is the purpose of programming detector sensitivity levels?
- A) Adjust volume
- B) Set detection threshold to environment (dusty, clean, humid)
- C) Change LED brightness
- D) Determine battery life

**Answer:** B) Set detection threshold appropriate for environment

---

**Q3.** What is a "zone" in fire alarm programming?
- A) A floor of the building
- B) A logical grouping of devices in a specific area for identification
- C) The panel enclosure
- D) Battery backup

**Answer:** B) A logical grouping of devices in a specific area for identification

---

### Intermediate Level

**Q4.** What programming creates a "cross-zone" verification sequence?
- A) Two zones in different buildings
- B) Requires two zones to alarm within time window before notification or suppression
- C) Zones on opposite walls
- D) Duplicate zone programming

**Answer:** B) Requires two zones to alarm within time window before notification

---

**Q5.** What is "positive alarm sequence" (PAS) per NFPA 72?
- A) Confirming real fire
- B) Allows 15-second investigation period before automatic notification, requires acknowledgment
- C) Alarm goes positive voltage
- D) Increasing alarm volume

**Answer:** B) 15-second initial period, 180-second investigation, auto-alarm if not reset

---

**Q6.** When programming elevator recall, what initiating devices trigger Phase I recall?
- A) All smoke detectors
- B) Lobby, machine room, and hoistway smoke detectors per ASME A17.1
- C) Pull stations only
- D) Heat detectors only

**Answer:** B) Lobby, machine room, and hoistway smoke detectors

---

### Advanced Level

**Q7.** What programming is required for HVAC shutdown integration?
- A) Not programmed, automatic
- B) Duct detector alarm triggers relay output to HVAC controls; may be zone-specific
- C) Telephone dialer
- D) NAC programming

**Answer:** B) Duct detector alarm triggers relay output to HVAC controls

---

**Q8.** What is "waterflow alarm delay" programming and what is the typical maximum permitted?
- A) Delay before activating pumps; no limit
- B) Delay before transmitting alarm signal; typically 90 seconds max per NFPA
- C) Delay before sprinklers activate
- D) Maintenance timer

**Answer:** B) Delay before transmitting; 90 seconds typical maximum (retard)

---

**Q9.** When programming a voice evacuation system, what is the recommended message priority order?
- A) Live voice, then recorded
- B) Alert tone, pre-recorded message, live voice capability
- C) All equal priority
- D) Written messages first

**Answer:** B) Alert tone, then pre-recorded evacuation message, with live voice override

---

**Q10.** How do you program "day/night mode" sensitivity for smoke detectors?
- A) Manual panel switch
- B) Time schedule adjusts detector sensitivity (tighter during occupied, looser unoccupied)
- C) Automatic based on light level
- D) Different detectors for each mode

**Answer:** B) Time schedule adjusts detector sensitivity levels
