## SECTION 1: CODES & STANDARDS (20 Questions)

### Entry Level

**Q1.** What does the acronym "DAS" stand for?
- A) Digital Audio System
- B) Distributed Antenna System
- C) Direct Access Signal
- D) Data Amplification Service

**Answer:** B) Distributed Antenna System

---

**Q2.** Which FCC Part governs public safety radio frequencies (700/800 MHz) used in DAS?
- A) FCC Part 15
- B) FCC Part 25
- C) FCC Part 90
- D) FCC Part 97

**Answer:** C) FCC Part 90

---

**Q3.** What does the IBC Section 510/911 require for high-rise buildings regarding radio coverage?
- A) Wi-Fi access
- B) Emergency responder radio coverage (ERRC) throughout the building
- C) Cellular signal for commercial carriers
- D) Cable TV distribution

**Answer:** B) Emergency responder radio coverage (ERRC)

---

**Q4.** What does "BDA" stand for in public safety communications?
- A) Building Data Amplifier
- B) Bi-Directional Amplifier
- C) Base Distribution Antenna
- D) Broadband Digital Adapter

**Answer:** B) Bi-Directional Amplifier

---

**Q5.** What does "ERRC" stand for in building codes?
- A) Emergency Radio Relay Cable
- B) Emergency Responder Radio Coverage
- C) Electronic Radio Reception Center
- D) Enhanced RF Relay Communication

**Answer:** B) Emergency Responder Radio Coverage

---

**Q6.** Who typically has final approval authority for public safety BDA/DAS systems?
- A) Building owner
- B) General contractor
- C) Authority Having Jurisdiction (AHJ) / Fire Marshal
- D) Telecommunications carrier

**Answer:** C) AHJ / Fire Marshal

## SECTION 2: INSTALLATION (20 Questions)

### Entry Level

**Q1.** When installing radiating (leaky) coax, what is the PRIMARY advantage over discrete antennas?
- A) Lower cost per foot
- B) Uniform signal distribution along the entire cable length
- C) Higher maximum power
- D) Easier troubleshooting

**Answer:** B) Uniform signal distribution along entire length

---

**Q2.** What tool is used to properly torque N-type or 7-16 DIN connectors?
- A) Adjustable wrench
- B) Calibrated torque wrench
- C) Channel-lock pliers
- D) Standard socket wrench

**Answer:** B) Calibrated torque wrench specific to connector type

---

**Q3.** Before pulling coaxial cable for DAS, what must be verified first?
- A) Cable has been factory tested
- B) Maximum pull tension and minimum bend radius
- C) Cable color
- D) All antennas already mounted

**Answer:** B) Maximum pull tension rating and minimum bend radius

---

**Q4.** What is a "donor antenna" in a BDA system?
- A) An indoor coverage antenna

- C) A backup antenna
- D) A testing antenna

**Answer:** B) Receiving antenna aimed at macro tower to capture signal

---

**Q5.** What is a "server antenna" in a BDA/DAS system?
- A) The outdoor antenna
- B) The indoor antenna that distributes signal to building occupants
- C) A network server component
- D) A backup component

**Answer:** B) Indoor antenna distributing signal to building occupants

---

**Q6.** What type of coaxial connector is commonly used in commercial DAS installations?
- A) BNC
- B) F-type

**Answer:** C) N-type or 7-16 DIN connectors

**Q13.** When installing surge protection for DAS, where should lightning/surge arrestors be placed?

## SECTION 3: COMPONENTS & FUNCTIONS (20 Questions)

### Entry Level

**Q1.** What is the function of the DAS "headend"?
- A) Connects to phones directly
- B) Receives, conditions, and distributes RF signals to remote antennas
- C) Stores recordings
- D) Provides internet

**Answer:** B) Receives, conditions, and distributes RF signals

---

**Q2.** What is a BDA and what role does it play?
- A) Only amplifies outgoing signals
- B) Amplifies both uplink and downlink signals for two-way communication
- C) Converts analog to digital
- D) Battery backup

**Answer:** B) Amplifies both uplink and downlink for two-way comms

---

**Q3.** What is the function of a "splitter" or "power divider" in DAS?
- A) Increases signal
- B) Divides RF signal into multiple paths with calibrated loss
- C) Converts frequencies
- D) Filters noise

**Answer:** B) Divides RF signal into multiple distribution paths

---

**Q4.** What is an "omnidirectional antenna" and where is it typically used?
- A) Aims signal in one direction
- B) Radiates signal in all horizontal directions (360°); used for general indoor coverage
- C) Only for outdoor use
- D) A testing tool

**Answer:** B) Radiates 360° horizontally; used for general indoor coverage

---

**Q5.** What is a "directional antenna" (panel or Yagi) used for?
- A) General coverage
- B) Focusing signal in a specific direction for targeted coverage or as a donor antenna
- C) Only indoor use
- D) Backup only

**Answer:** B) Focuses signal in specific direction for targeted coverage

---

**Q6.** What does the DAS amplify — the building's internal signals or external cell tower signals?
- A) Internal WiFi
- B) Both: external tower signals for indoor coverage (downlink) and indoor device signals back to tower (uplink)
- C) Only power signals
- D) Only voice calls

**Answer:** B) Both downlink (tower→indoor) and uplink (indoor→tower)

---

### Intermediate Level

**Q7.** What is the difference between passive DAS and active DAS?
- A) Passive uses batteries; active uses AC
- B) Passive uses coax/splitters; active uses fiber-fed remote units with amplification
- C) Passive is outdoor; active is indoor
- D) Passive is one carrier; active is multiple

**Answer:** B) Passive = coax/splitters; Active = fiber + remote units

---

**Q8.** What is the role of an "Optical Point of Interface" (OPI) in a system like CommScope ION-E?
- A) Provides power
- B) Converts optical signals to RF and distributes to remote antenna units
- C) Connects to fire alarm
- D) Stores configuration

**Answer:** B) Converts optical signals to RF for remote units

---

**Q9.** What is a "directional coupler" and how does it differ from a splitter?
- A) Amplifies signals
- B) Taps a small portion of signal while passing the majority through; splitter divides equally
- C) Filters frequencies
- D) Converts impedance

**Answer:** B) Taps small portion (e.g., -10dB) while passing most through

---

**Q10.** What is a "combiner" in DAS headend equipment?
- A) Signal analyzer
- B) Device that merges multiple frequency bands onto a single coaxial output for shared distribution
- C) Power supply
- D) A splitter in reverse

**Answer:** B) Merges multiple frequency bands onto shared distribution path

---

**Q11.** What is "radiating coax" (leaky feeder) and where is it typically deployed?
- A) Standard coax with connectors
- B) Coax with slots that radiate RF along its length; used in tunnels, corridors, parking garages
- C) Fiber optic cable
- D) High-power outdoor cable

**Answer:** B) Coax with slots radiating signal continuously; tunnels, corridors, garages

---

**Q12.** What is a "remote unit" (RU) in an active DAS?
- A) A control panel
- B) A floor-level or zone-level device that receives optical signal, converts to RF, amplifies, and feeds antennas
- C) A battery backup
- D) A testing instrument

**Answer:** B) Converts optical to RF, amplifies, and feeds local antennas

---

**Q13.** What is the purpose of "attenuators" in a DAS distribution system?
- A) Increase power
- B) Reduce signal level to balance coverage and prevent over-powering certain areas
- C) Filter noise

**Answer:** B) Reduce signal level for balanced coverage

## SECTION 4: PROGRAMMING & CONFIGURATION (20 Questions)

### Entry Level

**Q1.** When commissioning a BDA, what is the FIRST parameter to verify?
- A) Internet speed
- B) Donor signal strength from the macro site
- C) Number of connected devices
- D) Building occupancy

**Answer:** B) Donor signal strength from the macro site

---

**Q2.** What measurement unit is used for RF signal strength in DAS walk tests?
- A) Volts
- B) Watts
- C) dBm
- D) Hertz

**Answer:** C) dBm (decibels relative to one milliwatt)

---

**Q3.** When programming a BDA, what does "gain" refer to?
- A) Signal volume
- B) Amount of signal amplification in dB; too high causes oscillation
- C) Frequency range
- D) Number of devices

**Answer:** B) Signal amplification; excess causes oscillation

---

**Q4.** What is a "walk test" in DAS commissioning?
- A) A fitness test
- B) Systematic signal measurement throughout the building to verify coverage meets requirements
- C) Equipment transport
- D) Safety inspection

**Answer:** B) Systematic signal measurement to verify coverage meets specs

---

**Q5.** What instrument is used to perform DAS walk tests?
- A) Multimeter
- B) Signal analyzer or specialized walk test tool (PCTEL, iBwave)
- C) Oscilloscope
- D) Cable tester

**Answer:** B) Signal analyzer or walk test tool

---

**Q6.** What does "downlink" mean in DAS terminology?
- A) Signal going underground
- B) Signal from the macro tower/headend to the building occupants (receive signal)
- C) Signal going down in power
- D) Download speed

**Answer:** B) Signal from tower/headend to building occupants

---

### Intermediate Level

**Q7.** When performing a link budget calculation, which losses must be included?
- A) Cable loss, splitter loss, connector loss, and antenna gain
- B) Only cable loss and antenna gain
- C) Only splitter loss
- D) Cable loss only

**Answer:** A) Cable loss + splitter loss + connector loss + antenna gain

---

**Q8.** What is "isolation" in BDA context and what is the minimum recommended?
- A) Physical distance only; 10 feet
- B) Signal attenuation between donor and server antennas; must exceed gain by 15 dB
- C) Any amount acceptable
- D) Frequency separation; 25 MHz

**Answer:** B) Must exceed system gain by at least 15 dB

---

**Q9.** What is the purpose of the "composite signal" approach in multi-band DAS?
- A) Reduce racks
- B) Combine all frequency bands onto single distribution for shared infrastructure
- C) Eliminate band-specific antennas
- D) Increase output power

**Answer:** B) Combine all bands on shared distribution path

---

**Q10.** What does "uplink" mean in DAS terminology?
- A) Signal going up in power
- B) Signal from indoor mobile devices back to the macro tower (transmit signal)
- C) Upload speed
- D) Antenna mounting direction

**Answer:** B) Signal from indoor devices back to macro tower

---

**Q11.** What does "coupling loss" refer to in radiating coax specifications?
- A) Connector loss
- B) The signal level radiated per unit length from the leaky feeder cable to the surrounding area
- C) Cable bending loss
- D) Frequency drift

**Answer:** B) Signal radiated per unit length from leaky feeder to surrounding area

---

**Q12.** What is the purpose of "squelch" settings on a BDA?
- A) Reduce noise output
- B) Set threshold below which the amplifier does not amplify (prevents amplifying noise floor)
- C) Limit frequency range
- D) Control gain

**Answer:** B) Minimum threshold to prevent noise-floor amplification

---

**Q13.** When configuring DAS for both 700 MHz and 800 MHz public safety, what frequency planning is critical?
- A) Use same settings
- B) Separate gain and filter settings for each band to prevent intermodulation

### Advanced Level

**Q14.** Walk test reveals garage areas failing -95 dBm. Radiating coax has -4.5 dB/100ft coupling loss. What is the likely corrective action?
- A) Replace all coax
- B) Reduce spacing between runs or add supplemental antennas in dead spots
- C) Exceed FCC limits

**Q15.** PIM test shows -110 dBc 3rd-order at 851 MHz vs. -153 dBc spec. What is the likely source?
- A) Faulty amplifier
- B) Loose or improperly torqued connector
- C) Wrong antenna model
- D) Wrong cable type

**Answer:** B) Loose/improperly torqued connector (most common PIM source)

**Q16.** A 30-story building needs -75 dBm RSRP at cell edge. Headend output +27 dBm, path loss 82 dB. What antenna gain is needed?
- A) 0 dBi

---

**Q17.** After commissioning, Carrier A reports 6 dB uplink noise floor rise. What is the cause?
- A) Carrier macro fault
- B) DAS uplink gain too high, amplifying noise from all remote units
- C) Antenna aiming
- D) Fiber damage

**Answer:** B) Excessive UL gain aggregating noise from all remotes

---

**Q18.** What is "system oscillation" in a BDA and how is it prevented?
- A) Power cycling
- B) Feedback loop from server antenna back to donor antenna; prevented by ensuring isolation exceeds gain + 15 dB
- C) Frequency hopping
- D) Timer settings

**Answer:** B) Feedback loop; prevented by maintaining isolation > gain + 15 dB

---

**Q19.** How do you calculate the number of antennas needed for a 50,000 sq ft floor plate?

- C) Standard of 1 per 1000 sq ft

---

**Q20.** What is "inter-band isolation" in a multi-carrier DAS headend and why is it critical?

- B) RF isolation between different carrier bands to prevent signal leakage interference; critical for avoiding desense
- C) Distance between antennas
- D) Time separation between signals
