## SECTION 1: STANDARDS & CODES (20 Questions)

### Entry Level

**Q1.** What is the primary TIA standard governing commercial building telecommunications cabling?
- A) TIA-568
- B) TIA-606
- C) TIA-569
- D) TIA-607

**Answer:** A) TIA-568 (Generic Telecommunications Cabling)

---

**Q2.** According to TIA-568, what is the maximum total channel length for horizontal copper cabling?
- A) 90 meters
- B) 100 meters
- C) 295 feet
- D) 328 feet

**Answer:** B) 100 meters (90m permanent link + 10m patch cords)

---

**Q3.** What TIA standard covers telecommunications pathways and spaces (conduit, cable trays, rooms)?
- A) TIA-568
- B) TIA-569
- C) TIA-606
- D) TIA-607

**Answer:** B) TIA-569

---

**Q4.** What organization publishes the TDMM (Telecommunications Distribution Methods Manual)?
- A) IEEE
- B) BICSI
- C) ANSI
- D) NFPA

**Answer:** B) BICSI

---

**Q5.** What does ANSI stand for in the context of telecommunications standards?
- A) American National Systems Institute
- B) American National Standards Institute
- C) Advanced Networking Standards Interface
- D) Association of National Standard Inspectors

**Answer:** B) American National Standards Institute

---

**Q6.** According to TIA-568, what is the minimum number of telecommunications outlets required per work area?
- A) 1
- B) 2
- C) 3
- D) 4

**Answer:** B) 2 (one for data, one for voice)

### Entry Level

**Q1.** What does "Cat6A" stand for and what is its frequency rating?
- A) Category 6 Augmented; 500 MHz
- B) Category 6 Advanced; 400 MHz
- C) Category 6 Analog; 350 MHz
- D) Category 6A; 250 MHz

**Answer:** A) Category 6 Augmented; rated to 500 MHz

**Q2.** What is the difference between UTP and STP cable?
- A) UTP is underground, STP is standard
- B) UTP is unshielded; STP has shielding for EMI protection

- A) 26 AWG

## SECTION 4: TESTING (20 Questions)

### Entry Level

**Q1.** What is a "wire map" test and what does it verify?
- A) Physical cable routing
- B) Correct pin-to-pin connectivity and proper termination
- C) Cable length
- D) Signal strength

**Answer:** B) Correct pin-to-pin connectivity and termination

---

**Q2.** What test instrument is used to certify Cat6A installations?
- A) Multimeter
- B) Cable tester/certifier (Fluke DSX, etc.)
- C) Oscilloscope
- D) Tone generator

**Answer:** B) Cable certifier (Fluke DSX, IDEAL, etc.)

---

**Q3.** What does a "PASS" result on a cable certification test indicate?
- A) Cable is connected
- B) All measured parameters meet or exceed category specifications
- C) Cable is the correct length
- D) Cable has no visible damage

**Answer:** B) All parameters meet category specifications

---

**Q4.** What is a tone generator and probe used for?
- A) Certifying cable performance
- B) Tracing and identifying specific cables in a bundle or wall
- C) Measuring signal strength
- D) Testing fiber optics

**Answer:** B) Tracing and identifying specific cables

---

**Q5.** What does a "split pair" fault indicate in a wire map test?
- A) Cable is too long
- B) Conductors from different pairs are wired to the same pin, breaking twist pairing
- C) Cable is broken
- D) Wrong cable type

**Answer:** B) Conductors from different pairs wired to same pin position

---

**Q6.** What is the purpose of a visual fault locator (VFL) in fiber testing?
- A) Certify fiber links
- B) Locate breaks, tight bends, or bad connections by sending visible red laser light
- C) Measure insertion loss
- D) Test bandwidth

**Answer:** B) Locate breaks and bad connections with visible red light

---

### Intermediate Level

**Q7.** What does "NEXT" stand for and why is it tested?
- A) Next cable in sequence; for inventory
- B) Near-End Crosstalk; measures interference between wire pairs at transmit end
- C) Network extension testing
- D) New equipment crossover test

**Answer:** B) Near-End Crosstalk - interference between pairs

---

**Q8.** What is the difference between Tier 1 and Tier 2 fiber testing?
- A) Tier 1 is faster
- B) Tier 1 = insertion loss; Tier 2 = OTDR (length, events, reflections)
- C) Tier 2 is cheaper
- D) No significant difference

**Answer:** B) Tier 1 = loss testing; Tier 2 = OTDR diagnostics

---

**Q9.** If a cable fails for "length" on certification, what is the most likely cause?
- A) Wrong cable type
- B) Permanent link exceeds 90 meters
- C) Wrong test adapter
- D) Low battery

**Answer:** B) Permanent link exceeds 90m (or total channel >100m)

---

**Q10.** What is "delay skew" and why does it matter?
- A) Installation speed variance
- B) The difference in propagation delay between the fastest and slowest pairs; excessive skew causes data errors
- C) Test equipment calibration issue
- D) Power-on delay for switches

**Answer:** B) Propagation delay difference between pairs; causes data errors if excessive

---

**Q11.** What is a "permanent link" adapter vs. a "channel" adapter on a cable certifier?
- A) They are the same
- B) Permanent link tests wall-to-wall cabling only; channel tests include patch cords
- C) Channel adapter is cheaper
- D) Permanent link is for fiber only

**Answer:** B) Permanent link = infrastructure only; Channel = includes patch cords

---

**Q12.** What does "propagation delay" measure on a copper cable?
- A) How fast you can pull cable
- B) The time it takes for a signal to travel the length of the cable
- C) Connector insertion loss
- D) Ambient noise level

**Answer:** B) Time for signal to travel the cable length (measured in nanoseconds)

---

**Q13.** What is the proper reference method for Tier 1 fiber loss testing?
- A) No reference needed
- B) Set a reference baseline by connecting test reference cords and zeroing the loss
- C) Use factory defaults
- D) Measure cable weight

**Answer:** B) Set reference baseline by connecting test cords and zeroing

---

### Advanced Level

**Q14.** What does "ACR-F" (Attenuation to Crosstalk Ratio - Far End) measure?
- A) Cable resistance
- B) Margin between signal attenuation and far-end crosstalk noise
- C) Connector loss
- D) Cable diameter

**Answer:** B) Signal headroom over far-end crosstalk (formerly ELFEXT)

---

**Q15.** When an OTDR trace shows a large reflection spike followed by flat line, what is indicated?
- A) Good splice
- B) Cable break or unterminated end
- C) Normal connector
- D) Low signal

**Answer:** B) Cable break or unterminated/open end

---

**Q16.** What is "return loss" and what causes failures?
- A) Signal reflection due to impedance mismatches
- B) Power consumption
- C) Cable theft
- D) Grounding issues

**Answer:** A) Signal reflection from impedance mismatches (poor terminations, kinks)

---

**Q17.** During certification, a Cat6A cable passes all parameters but shows marginal "PSANEXT" (Power Sum Alien NEXT). What is the recommended action?
- A) Pass anyway; marginal is acceptable
- B) Re-route cable to increase separation from adjacent cables
- C) Use shorter patch cords
- D) Increase frequency

**Answer:** B) Improve cable separation; ANEXT is adjacent-cable crosstalk

---

**Q18.** What is the "dead zone" on an OTDR and how does it affect testing?
- A) Cable fault type
- B) The minimum distance from the OTDR where events cannot be resolved; use a launch fiber to compensate
- C) Maximum fiber distance
- D) Temperature-related issue

**Answer:** B) Minimum resolvable distance; use launch fiber to move past dead zone

---

**Q19.** What is PS-NEXT and how does it differ from standard NEXT?
- A) Same measurement
- B) Power Sum NEXT; measures combined crosstalk from all other pairs simultaneously vs. pair-to-pair
- C) Post-installation NEXT
- D) Peak Signal NEXT

**Answer:** B) Power Sum of crosstalk from all other pairs; more comprehensive than pair-to-pair NEXT

---

**Q20.** When conducting an acceptance test for a new cabling installation, what documentation deliverables are typically required?
- A) Only a bill of materials
- B) Certification test results, as-built drawings, labeling records, and warranty registration
- C) Only photos
- D) Verbal confirmation

**Answer:** B) Certification results, as-built drawings, labeling records, warranty registration
