## SECTION 1: STANDARDS & CODES (10 Questions)

### Entry Level

**Q1.** What is the primary TIA standard governing commercial building telecommunications cabling?
- A) TIA-568
- B) TIA-606
- C) TIA-569
- D) TIA-607

**Answer:** A) TIA-568 (Generic Telecommunications Cabling)

---

**Q2.** According to TIA-568, what is the maximum total channel length for horizontal copper cabling?
- A) 90 meters
- B) 100 meters
- C) 295 feet
- D) 328 feet

**Answer:** B) 100 meters (90m permanent link + 10m patch cords)

---

**Q3.** What TIA standard covers telecommunications pathways and spaces (conduit, cable trays, rooms)?
- A) TIA-568
- B) TIA-569
- C) TIA-606
- D) TIA-607

**Answer:** B) TIA-569

---

### Intermediate Level

**Q4.** What are the two T568 wiring schemes, and which is more commonly used in commercial installations?
- A) T568A and T568B; T568B is more common in commercial
- B) T568A and T568B; T568A is more common
- C) T568C and T568D
- D) TIA-A and TIA-B

**Answer:** A) T568A and T568B; T568B more common commercially

---

**Q5.** According to TIA-568, what is the maximum length of the permanent link in a horizontal run?
- A) 100 meters
- B) 90 meters
- C) 80 meters
- D) 75 meters

**Answer:** B) 90 meters

---

**Q6.** What TIA standard governs administration (labeling) of telecommunications infrastructure?
- A) TIA-568
- B) TIA-569
- C) TIA-606
- D) TIA-607

**Answer:** C) TIA-606 (Administration Standard)

---

### Advanced Level

**Q7.** What NEC article specifically addresses communications circuits and their installation requirements?
- A) NEC Article 725
- B) NEC Article 770
- C) NEC Article 800
- D) NEC Article 820

**Answer:** C) NEC Article 800 (Communications Circuits)

---

**Q8.** According to TIA-568.2-D, what is the maximum insertion loss allowed for a Cat6A channel at 500 MHz?
- A) 20.1 dB
- B) 30.1 dB
- C) 50.1 dB
- D) 70.1 dB

**Answer:** B) Approximately 30.1 dB for Cat6A at 500 MHz

---

**Q9.** What TIA standard covers grounding and bonding for telecommunications infrastructure?
- A) TIA-568
- B) TIA-606
- C) TIA-607
- D) TIA-758

**Answer:** C) TIA-607 (Grounding and Bonding)

---

**Q10.** Per BICSI/TIA guidelines, what is the recommended maximum fill ratio for conduit containing data cables?
- A) 25%
- B) 40%
- C) 53%
- D) 100%

**Answer:** B) 40% maximum fill for ease of pulling

## SECTION 2: MATERIALS (10 Questions)

### Entry Level

**Q1.** What does "Cat6A" stand for and what is its frequency rating?
- A) Category 6 Augmented; 500 MHz
- B) Category 6 Advanced; 400 MHz
- C) Category 6 Analog; 350 MHz
- D) Category 6A; 250 MHz

**Answer:** A) Category 6 Augmented; rated to 500 MHz

---

**Q2.** What is the difference between UTP and STP cable?
- A) UTP is underground, STP is standard
- B) UTP is unshielded; STP has shielding for EMI protection
- C) STP is thinner
- D) No difference

**Answer:** B) UTP = Unshielded Twisted Pair; STP = Shielded

---

**Q3.** What does "plenum-rated" cable designation (CMP) indicate?
- A) Outdoor rated
- B) Low-smoke, low-flame spread for air-handling spaces
- C) High voltage rated
- D) Water resistant

**Answer:** B) Low-smoke, low-flame for air-handling spaces

---

### Intermediate Level

**Q4.** What fiber optic cable designation indicates single-mode outdoor-rated fiber?
- A) OM1
- B) OM4
- C) OS2
- D) OM5

**Answer:** C) OS2 (Single-mode low water peak)

---

**Q5.** What is the difference between LC and SC fiber connectors?
- A) LC is larger
- B) LC is smaller form factor; SC is larger "push-pull" style
- C) SC is newer technology
- D) No difference

**Answer:** B) LC = smaller duplex, SC = larger square connector

---

**Q6.** What does "riser-rated" (CMR) cable indicate for installation?
- A) Suitable for air-handling spaces
- B) Suitable for vertical runs between floors, not plenums
- C) Outdoor use
- D) Underground only

**Answer:** B) Suitable for vertical runs between floors (not air-handling)

---

### Advanced Level

**Q7.** What distinguishes OM3 from OM4 multimode fiber?
- A) Core diameter
- B) OM4 supports longer distances at 10Gb and higher bandwidth
- C) Connector type
- D) Color coding

**Answer:** B) OM4 = better bandwidth, longer 10G/40G/100G distances

---

**Q8.** What is "LSZH" cable jacketing and when is it required?
- A) Underground cable; always required
- B) Low Smoke Zero Halogen; required in enclosed spaces (transit, ships, data centers)
- C) Waterproof jacket
- D) Armored cable

**Answer:** B) Low Smoke Zero Halogen; enclosed occupancies

---

**Q9.** What is the typical attenuation specification for OS2 single-mode fiber at 1310nm?
- A) 3.5 dB/km
- B) 0.35 dB/km
- C) 1.0 dB/km
- D) 0.5 dB/km

**Answer:** B) Approximately 0.35 dB/km at 1310nm

---

**Q10.** What cable specification is required for 10GBASE-T over 100 meters?
- A) Cat5e
- B) Cat6 (with limitations)
- C) Cat6A
- D) Cat7

**Answer:** C) Cat6A (Cat6 limited to 55m for 10GBASE-T)

## SECTION 3: INSTALLATION (10 Questions)

### Entry Level

**Q1.** What is the minimum bend radius for Cat6 cable during installation?
- A) 1× cable diameter
- B) 4× cable diameter
- C) 8× cable diameter
- D) No minimum

**Answer:** B) 4× cable diameter (no load)

---

**Q2.** What is the correct color sequence for T568B termination?
- A) Green-White, Green, Orange-White, Blue, Blue-White, Orange, Brown-White, Brown
- B) Orange-White, Orange, Green-White, Blue, Blue-White, Green, Brown-White, Brown
- C) Blue-White, Blue, Green-White, Orange, Orange-White, Green, Brown-White, Brown
- D) Any order if consistent

**Answer:** B) Orange-White, Orange, Green-White, Blue, Blue-White, Green, Brown-White, Brown

---

**Q3.** What tool is used to terminate cable onto a 110-style patch panel?
- A) Crimping tool
- B) Punch-down tool
- C) Wire stripper
- D) Soldering iron

**Answer:** B) Punch-down tool (110 blade)

---

### Intermediate Level

**Q4.** What is the maximum pulling tension for 4-pair UTP cable?
- A) 10 lbf
- B) 25 lbf
- C) 50 lbf
- D) 100 lbf

**Answer:** B) 25 lbf (110N)

---

**Q5.** When installing cables in J-hooks, what is the typical maximum spacing?
- A) 24 inches
- B) 48-60 inches
- C) 10 feet
- D) No standard

**Answer:** B) 48-60 inches (4-5 feet) typical per manufacturer/TIA-569

---

**Q6.** Why must cables be de-twisted no more than 1/2 inch (13mm) at termination for Cat6?
- A) Aesthetic reasons
- B) Maintain twist ratio to prevent NEXT and FEXT crosstalk
- C) Easier termination
- D) Color coding

**Answer:** B) Maintain twist ratio to prevent crosstalk

---

### Advanced Level

**Q7.** When splicing single-mode fiber, what is the maximum acceptable splice loss?
- A) 0.5 dB
- B) 0.3 dB
- C) 0.1 dB (target <0.05 dB)
- D) 1.0 dB

**Answer:** C) 0.1 dB maximum; quality fusion <0.05 dB

---

**Q8.** What is "ANEXT" (Alien Near-End Crosstalk) and how is it mitigated?
- A) Crosstalk within cable; use shielding
- B) Crosstalk between adjacent cables; proper separation, use Cat6A/shielded
- C) Far-end noise
- D) Power cable interference

**Answer:** B) Crosstalk between adjacent cables; require proper spacing or Cat6A

---

**Q9.** When installing fiber in a cable tray, what is the minimum bend radius under no load?
- A) 2× cable diameter
- B) 10× cable diameter
- C) 15× cable diameter
- D) 50mm

**Answer:** B) Typically 10× cable OD (no tension); check manufacturer specs

---

**Q10.** What is the purpose of bonding metallic cable trays to the telecommunications grounding busbar?
- A) Improve signal quality
- B) Provide safety ground and equipotential bonding per TIA-607
- C) Required for power only
- D) Optional for performance

**Answer:** B) Safety ground and equipotential bonding per TIA-607

## SECTION 4: TESTING (10 Questions)

### Entry Level

**Q1.** What is a "wire map" test and what does it verify?
- A) Physical cable routing
- B) Correct pin-to-pin connectivity and proper termination
- C) Cable length
- D) Signal strength

**Answer:** B) Correct pin-to-pin connectivity and termination

---

**Q2.** What test instrument is used to certify Cat6A installations?
- A) Multimeter
- B) Cable tester/certifier (Fluke DSX, etc.)
- C) Oscilloscope
- D) Tone generator

**Answer:** B) Cable certifier (Fluke DSX, IDEAL, etc.)

---

**Q3.** What does a "PASS" result on a cable certification test indicate?
- A) Cable is connected
- B) All measured parameters meet or exceed category specifications
- C) Cable is the correct length
- D) Cable has no visible damage

**Answer:** B) All parameters meet category specifications

---

### Intermediate Level

**Q4.** What does "NEXT" stand for and why is it tested?
- A) Next cable in sequence; for inventory
- B) Near-End Crosstalk; measures interference between wire pairs at transmit end
- C) Network extension testing
- D) New equipment crossover test

**Answer:** B) Near-End Crosstalk - interference between pairs

---

**Q5.** What is the difference between Tier 1 and Tier 2 fiber testing?
- A) Tier 1 is faster
- B) Tier 1 = insertion loss; Tier 2 = OTDR (length, events, reflections)
- C) Tier 2 is cheaper
- D) No significant difference

**Answer:** B) Tier 1 = loss testing; Tier 2 = OTDR diagnostics

---

**Q6.** If a cable fails for "length" on certification, what is the most likely cause?
- A) Wrong cable type
- B) Permanent link exceeds 90 meters
- C) Wrong test adapter
- D) Low battery

**Answer:** B) Permanent link exceeds 90m (or total channel >100m)

---

### Advanced Level

**Q7.** What does "ACR-F" (Attenuation to Crosstalk Ratio - Far End) measure?
- A) Cable resistance
- B) Margin between signal attenuation and far-end crosstalk noise
- C) Connector loss
- D) Cable diameter

**Answer:** B) Signal headroom over far-end crosstalk (formerly ELFEXT)

---

**Q8.** When an OTDR trace shows a large reflection spike followed by flat line, what is indicated?
- A) Good splice
- B) Cable break or unterminated end
- C) Normal connector
- D) Low signal

**Answer:** B) Cable break or unterminated/open end

---

**Q9.** What is "return loss" and what causes failures?
- A) Signal reflection due to impedance mismatches
- B) Power consumption
- C) Cable theft
- D) Grounding issues

**Answer:** A) Signal reflection from impedance mismatches (poor terminations, kinks)

---

**Q10.** During certification, a Cat6A cable passes all parameters but shows marginal "PSANEXT" (Power Sum Alien NEXT). What is the recommended action?
- A) Pass anyway; marginal is acceptable
- B) Re-route cable to increase separation from adjacent cables
- C) Use shorter patch cords
- D) Increase frequency

**Answer:** B) Improve cable separation; ANEXT is adjacent-cable crosstalk
